How to read this page
Market breadth — how many individual stocks rose vs. fell. Strong breadth (most stocks up) suggests a healthy, broad rally; weak breadth alongside index gains can mean the move is being carried by just a few large names.
Sector rotation — money moving from one sector into another (e.g. out of Tech and into Energy) often signals a shift in investor risk appetite before it shows up in the headline indices.
BTC / ETH dominance — the share of the total crypto market cap held by Bitcoin or Ethereum. Rising dominance usually means capital is consolidating into the larger, more established coins (a risk-off signal within crypto).
Fear & Greed Index — a 0-100 sentiment score built from volatility, momentum, and social data. Extreme readings in either direction have historically (not reliably) preceded turning points.
10-Year Treasury yield — the return on U.S. government debt; a key benchmark for borrowing costs across the economy. Rising yields can pressure stock valuations, especially growth stocks.
Day / 24h range — the low-to-high price swing over the period, a quick read on how volatile trading has been.
